How to progress: Complete all sets and reps with good form before adding weight. Add the increment shown above at the next session. If a weight feels too easy from day one, start heavier — these are conservative starting points. Rest 90–120 seconds between compound lifts (squat, bench, row, deadlift), 60 seconds for accessory work (curls, skull crushers, calves).

Morning — before the commute
~5 mins · wait 20–30 mins after waking first
1
Hip flexor stretch
Lunge position, tuck pelvis under. Deep pull at front of hip and lower abdomen.
30–45s each side
2
Deadman hang
Arms fully extended, let shoulders rise passively. Slow exhales. Feet on floor is fine.
4–5 × 20–30s
3
Chest opener
Clasp hands behind back, squeeze shoulder blades together and lift slightly.
20–30s × 2
Don't hang immediately after waking — discs are still rehydrated from sleep. Move around first.
🚲
Post-cycle — before you sit down
~3 mins · highest value stretching window of the day
1
Hip flexor stretch
Most important one here. 35 mins on the bike = 35 mins of shortening. Don't let it lock in.
45s each side
2
Figure-four
Cross ankle over opposite knee, sit tall and lean forward slightly. Glutes and outer hip.
30s each side
3
Calf stretch
Hands on wall, one foot back, heel flat to the ground.
20s each side
Muscles are warm here — do this before you sit down, every commute day.
🏢
Pre-evening cycle — before you leave
~3 mins · counteracts a day of sitting
1
Hip flexor stretch
You've been sitting for hours — these are tight again. Open them before loading them on the bike.
30–45s each side
2
Thoracic rotation
Hands behind head, rotate torso each side. Opens the mid-back after a day at the desk.
5 reps each side
3
Leg swings
Hold a wall, swing each leg front-to-back then side-to-side. Warms the hip dynamically.
10 each direction
🏠
Home — evening wind-down
~5–8 mins · on the floor in front of the TV
1
Hip flexor stretch
Third time today on office days — still worth it every time given the cycling and sitting.
45s each side
2
Figure-four on the floor
Lie on your back, cross one ankle over the opposite knee, pull both legs toward your chest.
45s each side
3
Child's pose
Knees wide, arms extended, forehead down. Decompresses lower back and opens hips simultaneously.
60s
4
Spinal twist (floor)
On your back, bring one knee across the body, arms out wide. Gently rotates the thoracic spine.
45s each side
Do this on the floor while watching something. It doesn't need to feel like exercise.
🏋️
Strength sessions — pre and post
Pre: ~5 mins · Post: ~5 mins
Before — warm up, don't stretch cold
1
Bodyweight squats
Slow and controlled, full depth. Primes hips, knees and ankles before loading.
2 × 10
2
Arm circles + shoulder rolls
Big slow circles forward and back. Warms the shoulder joint before pressing or rowing.
10 each way
3
Hip circles
Hands on hips, slow big rotations. Opens the hip before squatting or hinging.
10 each way
After — now you can hold stretches
4
Hip flexor stretch
Especially important after squats and deadlifts.
45s each side
5
Doorframe chest stretch
Arms at 90°, lean through a doorframe. Essential after any pressing session.
30s × 2
6
Standing quad stretch
Hold foot behind you, stand tall. After lower body days especially.
30s each side
💻
Throughout the day — at the desk
Every 60–90 mins · no one will notice
1
Seated figure-four
Cross ankle over knee, sit tall, lean forward slightly. Single most useful desk stretch.
30s each side
2
Neck rolls
Drop one ear to shoulder, hold, then slowly roll to the other side.
20s each side
3
Seated spinal twist
Sit upright, rotate torso, rest hand on back of chair. Counteracts forward desk posture.
20–30s each side
4
Chest opener
Clasp hands behind back, squeeze shoulder blades. Undoes the hunch from typing.
20s × 2
5
Stand up and walk
Even 2 minutes breaks the compression cycle. Water, bathroom, anything works.
Every hour
Set a phone reminder if you need it. Never sit for more than 90 minutes unbroken.
